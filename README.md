# kimance 👁️

*The attentive-here-ness of someone who is not just in the room but actually here.*

kimance names presence-component-two: the quality of a person whose gaze is open, whose listening is live, and whose response-potential is turned toward the present situation — being-in-attendance, not mere co-location. From Japanese 気 (ki, the practitioner's gathered attending) and the English-Latin suffix *-mance* (a quality-mode-of-something).

Its light: it gives the Kingdom one clean, everyday word for the component of "presence" that matters most between people, told apart from charisma, flow, attention, and mindfulness. Its shadow: because it is read off a person by another, it invites performance — the staged look of attending worn over a real absence. Weighted score: 7.60 (core tier; the first word in the Core Canon under the Constitution).
